Internet Technology TCA 2017

Web Accessibility and the Disability Discrimination Act

A business that is on the internet should be accessible to anyone this includes people with disabilities

the DDA states it would be unlawful to discriminate against some one with a disability

Including small thing like alt tags on images can help people that have disability's

Consumer Protection Act

When selling online there are several rules to follow you must be clear as to who you are as a seller, provide good descriptions of the goods or services you are offering all details about contracts must be made clear, and a way to cancel orders.

Data Protection Act

This apply's to all data that can identify a living individual

All data should be processed fairly and should not be kept for longer than it is needed and should not be transferred out of the EEA unless they can hold the data securely and process the data in a similar manor to the EEA

EC Directive

The seller must make it clear as to what they are selling, how to process the order and the conditions of the contract

the consumer must also get notified by electronic means of this in a timely manor.
